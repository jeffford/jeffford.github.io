A bema was a platform for public addresses that stood in the forum marketplace of Greek cities. The bema of Corinth, called a "tribunal" in Acts, was built about 44 C.E. of blue and white marble.
Acts tells of Paul being brought before the tribunal (bema). The rock in the backgorund is a monolithic rock overseeing the ancient city of Corinth.

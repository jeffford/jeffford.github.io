New Orleans has more ironwork than other U.S. cities, thanks to a group of citizens dedicated to the preservation of the old French Quarter. Its earliest ironwork was forged by Spanish and French smiths. Unfortunately, fires, rust, and remodeling have so taken their toll of the Spanish ironwork that almost the only remaining example of importance is the gateway of the Cabildo.

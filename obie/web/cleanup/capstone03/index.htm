How should you choose a project?

There is no one way to choose a project. However, first and foremost, your project should have the following characteristics:

1. It interests you as you will be spending a lot of time working on it.

2. It allows you to show off your computer science knowledge AND your management knowledge. In fact, we will be looking for both components in your project and that is why there is a contact person from both departments.

3. Just talking about something isn't sufficient. We expect you to implement something - deploy software, deploy hardware, implement a data base, etc.

4. We expect you to reflect on what you are doing and why you are doing it.

Note that strictly web designs have not been good choices as anyone can design using tools such as Visual Page. If you are thinking about doing some kind of web design, be sure you are doing something complex with it such as interfacing it to a database.

Some abstracts are provided below from some past projects. Because of privacy concerns, I can't give grades for these, but we can chat with you about appropriateness if you want to do something that is related to any of these.

While the project (or some part of a larger project of which you are a part) is to be your work, this does not mean that we won't try to provide some help if you need it. Just allow for time for us to research a possible answer as all of you will be working on diverse topics.

Your deliverables for this course will include : Note: These are NOT in order of the due dates.

I. A formal proposal for your project. Send as an attachment by email to each of the 3 contact persons above. See details in the Reference section below. Must be approved by the end of January. As most proposals require several rewrites and clairfications, please interact with all 3 of us early about your possible ideas for projects.


II. An abstract of your project - Due two days before your presentation. The provided examples of past projects are the abstracts submitted by the capstone participants so you should see many examples of the desired style. Please check grammar and spelling as they are provided to a diverse audience at the time of your presentation.

III. A paper written in a professional research style. Due two days before your presentation. Details about the paper are given in the Reference section below.

IV. Maintain a journal during your project about what you are doing, why you are doing it, what problems you encountered, what help you required etc. This journal will be very helpful when you write your paper. We would also like to have a copy of the journal submitted with your paper.

V. A talk of 20 minutes in length. There will be 10 minutes for questioning after your talk. Be sure to organize your talk so you can pack a lot of information AND a short demo into the time. The talks will be one night in the 12th week of the semester. The exact date and time will be chosen later. You may invite friends, families, employers, and anyone else to the presentation. Refreshments are always served.

VI. Burn 3 CDs containing all of the above so they can be distributed to Obie, Ellen, and Jane. You should hand in two hard copies of the paper so we can return it to you with comments after all three of us have graded it.

Realize that you will spend a lot more time on the capstone than your credit hours will reflect. We are very much aware of this, but we were trying to keep costs to you down. As a capstone, this is your time to show off how much you have learned in your various courses and bring it all together in one project.

Some of you will use projects at your place of work. However, realize that equally good projects have been stand alone ones when the individual sought out a problem and then solved it for someone. You do need to think about the management aspect that is required so you can't just do something for yourself --- such as a web page for your hobby or a data base for some collection of yours.

CAPSTONE- Computer Science Requirements

  • Contact person: Dr. Oberta A. Slotterbeck (obie@hiram.edu)

    Your deliverables for this course will include :

      A formal proposal for your project.

    This must be approximately a 2 page discussion of how you envision your project. Explain
    1) What you plan to implement- to the best of your knowledge right now.
    2) Why this project is of interest to you.
    3) Specify any web sites or journal articles you have explored that are about your topic.
    4) Describe how you plan to implement the project in stages.
    5) How your computer science and management skills will be utilized.
    6) How your project can be judged as successful.

    Many of you have chatted with me informally about possible projects. Almost all informal proposals needed some refinement and massaging. If you haven't interacted with me at all about possible ideas, I suggest you do so quickly. A mail message is fine.

    For those doing the capstone this term, I will email your formal proposals to the management professors for feedback although it would be good to bounce your ideas off of Dean Rose, also. There may be several revisions required for the proposal even though the basic idea is approved already. For those scheduling capstones in the future, you must schedule a meeting with someone both in the management department and in our department prior to the term in which you plan to do the work. For our department, email contact may take the place of a face-to-face meeting. Contact Dean Rose about details concerning the management department and who to contact. Management may have some additional requirements.

      An abstract of your project - Due two days before your presentation
      A paper written in a journal style- Due two days before your presentation.
      A talk of 20 minutes in length. There will be 10 minutes for questioning. Be sure to organize your talk so you can pack a lot of information AND a short demo in to the time.
